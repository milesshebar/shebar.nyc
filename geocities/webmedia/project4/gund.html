What if construction could happen without opposition?
The Problem

The Kenyon New Residences Association (NRA) came to us with a problem: people were unhappy with Gambier's increasing construction. The impending destruction of residential buildings like Farr Hall, Bushnell, and Manning, and the relocation of businesses like the Village Market and temporary closing of the Gambier Deli had been met with anger and bewilderment. Graham Gund, the architectural mastermind behind all these developments, was widely criticized.

The Proposal

Rarefaction proposed a pro-Graham Gund campaign targeted at supporters of the NRA to garner financial support for Gund Rights lobbyists.

The Effect

Support for Gund Rights saw unprecedented growth rates and the NRA received contributions domestically, internationally, and parentally. A new Black Box theater was opened, construction began on the new Market and several additional apartment-style buildings, and Farr Hall is slated to be torn down this summer. Well done, team.
