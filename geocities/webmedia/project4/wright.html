What if Mount Vernon had its own Kenyon?

Funny you should ask. Because we already did it. Boasting classrooms, screening rooms, and studio spaces, the Wright Center is the next step for Kenyon's film program and first step in interacting with life off the Hill. Because if there was a way to solve a town and gown issue, this was definitely it.

The History

Originally the Buckeye Candy and Tobacco Company's factory, the historic building that the Wright Center now occupies isn't just a ton of bricks -- it's an Ohio landmark. We designed custom paintwork and lettering for the building's exterior, keeping it stylistically faithful to the original but distinctive enough to remind residents of Mount Vernon that the building is no longer theirs.

The Building

Oh yeah, we totally modded the inside. Rarefaction designed the layout for the building's interior, replete with all the amenities you'd expect from professional post-production houses. The top two floors are devoted to the Film department, boasting equipment rooms, color and sound suites, and classrooms big and small. The bottom floor has some kind of like community outreach thing or something.
